How to Clarify the Problem?

What does "problem" mean?

Perodua defines a problem as:
“Gap between an Ideal Situation and the Current Situation”.

Step 1: Clarify the Problem

① Clarify Ultimate Goal

State your goal.
What are you seeking to achieve?

② Clarify Ideal Situation

If everything is resolved, what is the ideal situation?



In contrast to the ideal situation, what is the current situation?

③ Clarify using facts

Clarify using facts through Genchi Genbutsu.

④ Visualize the problem

Visualize problem to make everyone understand.

⑤ Clarify the impact

Clarify impact to job performance, customer, & business organization.

Example of Clarifying the Problem

① Ultimate Goal
1 Billion RM in annual sales

Problem statement: Sales not achieved

② Ideal Situation

Sales: 10 million RM
③ Current Situation
Sales: 7 million RM
(consider the same timeline – annually, monthly, etc.)
Using facts thru GENCHI GENBUTSU
④ Clarify the impact to job performance, customer, business organization.
● Not meeting sales quota
● Customer long waiting time
● Profit/loss/bonus
"Problem is clarified"
3 million RM short in sales
Indicate by specific GAP


Step 1: Details of the Process and Key Points

Clarify the Ultimate Goal of one's responsibilities and work.

● See the connection between the company’s Ultimate Goal and one’s job.

Clarify the Ideal Situation of
one’s work.

● Consider the Ideal Situation of one’s work in terms of “Who does it”- “What is it” – “How Much” – “When” – “What condition.

Clarify the Current Situation.

● It is important to investigate facts thru Genchi Genbutsu with one’s own eyes and examine objective facts.

Visualize the gap and view the problem.

● Clarify the gap between the Current Situation and the Ideal Situation and visualize what is wrong in the Current Situation for everyone to understand. The gap is the problem.

Clarify the impact to business.

● Impact includes those affecting one’s job, the customers and the organization.
