Step 4: Details of the Process and Key Points

Examine the Point of Occurrence and think of possible causes without any prejudice (assumption)
■ Raises all the possible causes from various perspectives without having any preconceived ideas to find out why the problem occurred.
■ Identify all possible direct causes by imaging the situation of the Genba, actually seeing the Genba, thinking of the root cause.

Continue to question “WHY” and identify the cause
■ Confirm the facts of the possible causes at the Genba through Genchi Genbutsu
■ Continue asking “WHY” based on facts and clarify the cause-and-result sequence

Specify the root cause
■ Keep asking why and find the root cause
■ Check weather the cause is a fact and weather it is the true cause.

“What is the cause of poor sales negotiation of a staff member at the dealer?”

O Situation 1

(WHY 1?) => The staff member does not make an effort

(WHY 2?) => His motivation is low

(WHY 3?) => He does not have confidence

(WHY 4?) => He does not know to negotiate with customers

(WHY 5?) => There are no negotiation guidelines for staff members

The ROOT CAUSE !!

X Situation 2

(WHY 1?) => The staff member does not make an effort

(WHY 2?) => His motivation is low

(WHY 3?) => Their salary is low

(WHY 4?) => Performance evaluation skills of their supervisor is low => Spread out!

(WHY 5?) => Performance evaluation system is not good => x


The focus shifted from staff, then the supervisor and then the system (Spread Out).
It may be true that “Performance evaluation skill of their supervisor is low”, however, the focus of the root cause analysis should be on the problem with the staff and we need to question things such as performance levels or work attitudes to find out the root cause.
